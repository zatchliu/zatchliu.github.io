The assignment was to create a biologically-inspired mechanism which would hop after a delay of at 8-60 seconds. The design had to include a timing mechanism, which when activated, triggered a hopping mechanism following a delay. We were also required to develop a personal design goal at the beginning of the project.

**Materials**

The design was restricted to certain materials. We were given a kit with the items we were able to use, and had access to various other resources, such as adhesives, rocket ignitor, 3d printing, and medium-density fiberboard (MDF) to be used in the laser cutter. There was no limitation on laser-cut MDF.

**Design Goals and Preliminary Sketches**

I was inspired by froghoppers, particularly the way it haphazardly launches itself into and twirls and flips in the air. I decided my design goal would be for my hopper to flip and land upright, like the froghopper. I realized that the biggest hurdle would probably be the timing mechanism. Early on in my design I decided to use the balloon as my timer, which would eventually remain the same in the final design. Another aspect of my design that would remain unchanged is the use of the spring and this v-shaped geometry as seen in the sketches. My focus at this stage was producing ways to redistribute weight and how to executed my design

**Prototype I**

The first prototype was constructed in the first few studio sessions leading up to the first design review. It was crudely built from 1/4in dowels held together my masking tape and zip ties. The two torsion springs sat on a dowel with the legs taped and zip tied to the longer dowels. The mechanism works by rotating the legs so that the shorter legs pass through the gap between the longer legs and the spring is over extended. The balloon is then put between the two legs and once the balloon deflates enough the legs will spring back, launching the hopper. The overall mechanism would remain unchanged for the future iterations.

The balloon was zip tied to a plastic tube to slow down the rate at which it would deflate. A wire was also inserted into the tube to further slow it down  


**Prototype II**

The main feedback I received from my first design meeting was to construct a more robust model from MDF and to experiment with the size. I took this advice and constructed the second prototype out of MDF and scaled down to about half the original size. Some other minor adjustments were adding some feet to the hopper, giving the balloon room to sit about the table when its inflated, and thus stabilizing the hopper. The wooden dowel that connected the two springs was replaced with a rubber tube with a 1/8in dowel inside of it. This was done to add friction so that the zip ties that secured the springs wouldn't move but still allowed the springs to freely rotate.

**Development**

The functionality of the hopper was working well at this stage, but I was ideating ways to create more vertical height rather than forward. I experimented with creating legs out of 1/8 in MDF instead of 1/4 in. The idea was to reduce the weight to get some more vertical height from the hopper. However, I quickly realized this wasn't going to work as the 1/8in MDF would warp as I began to rotate it around the springs. I decided to stick with the 1/4 in MDF.

A flaw that came up during my second design review was that after repeated use, hairline cracks would start to appear near the base of the legs where the springs were attached. To combat this, I drafted a new design for the lefts that would be used to the next prototype.

**Prototype III and IV**

The third iteration remained largely the same as the previous prototype. With the only adjustment being the new reinforced legs. However, I found these new leg to still lead to tiny cracks, now in the other direction.

This fourth iteration further reinforced the legs. I also added bracing to the short leg to help with force being applied to both directions of the legs. The bracing wasn't possible on the longer legs as it would then prevent the shorter legs from rotating completely. I ultimately didn't go with this design for the hopper celebration as the extras weight from the bracing caused the detriment of the hoppers flipping and landing capabilities.
